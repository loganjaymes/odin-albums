S.C.I.E.N.C.E.

Description

S.C.I.E.N.C.E. is the sophomore album by the band Incubus, released on September 9, 1997. The album was completed in six weeks, and recorded at a small studio in Santa Monica. This album was the band's first full-length album done with a major label.

The album itself blends a multitude of metal subgenres, most notably alternative-metal, nu-metal, and funk-metal, but also incorporates elements of hip-hop, jazz, and electronic music. Alongside this, the record was described by Rock Cellar Magazine as a "weed-and-mushrooms influenced funk/metal freakout".
